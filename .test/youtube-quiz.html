The Post-Dispatch Weatherbird is the oldest continuously running daily cartoon in the U.S.

Take our quiz and find out how much you know about the Bird and St. Louis' rich history in comics.

The Weatherbird hatched on what date?
Wrong.Right. It was too cold for words that day, but the Weatherbird began to talk later. At first he chirped only about weather, but soon he was offering pithy opinions about news issues.
Which of these Post-Dispatch artists created the Weatherbird?
Wrong.Right. Harry B. Martin (at left in the photo) called it the "little Dickey bird." The first of three Martins to draw the Weatherbird, Harry drew the cartoon from 1901-1903.
The Weatherbird became a brand name for what kind of product in 1907?
Wrong.Right. The Peters Shoe Co. sold Weatherbird School Shoes. But in 1931, the newspaper acquired a Weatherbird patent, so the shoe company switched to a rooster.
Which famous jazz musician wrote a salute to the Weatherbird?
Wrong.Right. Armstrong wrote the "Weatherbird Rag" which was recorded by New Orleans jazz great King Oliver in 1923.
Which local gangster gave himself the nickname "the Weatherbird"?
Wrong.Right. Russo was arrested in 1940 with John Vitale on drug charges. Whether he got his alias from the Post-Dispatch cartoon is not known.
When did the Weatherbird kick his tobacco habit?
Wrong.Right. The Weatherbird was frequently depicted with a cigar in his mouth from 1901 until newspaper management declared smoking politically incorrect in 1987.
What comic strip was inspired by a musical comedy that the strip's creator saw as a boy in St. Louis' Grand Opera House?
Wrong.Right. George McManus' influential strip centers around Jiggs, an Irishman who becomes wealthy after winning a sweepstakes, but longs for his old working-class lifestyle.
Chic Young, who grew up in St. Louis, created which of these comic strips in 1930?
Wrong.Right. Young was born in Chicago but grew in up St. Louis, where he attended William McKinley High School.
Which of these superheroes debuted in 1936, two years before Superman was first published?
Wrong.Right. "The Phantom" was created by St. Louisan Lee Falk, who also created "Mandrake the Magician." Both comics continue today.
Famous slacker "Beetle Bailey" was developed by Mort Walker while he was a student at which college?
Wrong.Right. Though Walker attended Wash. U. for a year, and though he grew up in K.C., it was while he attended Mizzou that he created the uber-popular "Beetle Bailey" strip.
Which of these popular comic-strip dogs was created by a CBC grad?
Wrong.Right. CBC grad Mike Peters began "Mother Goose and Grimm" in 1984 and still draws it today.
Glenn McCoy, creator of "The Duplex" lives where?
Wrong.Right. McCoy lives in his hometown, Belleville, where he began "The Duplex" in 1993. However, "Popeye" creator Elzie Segar was born in Chester.
